TIMELINE (VERY COMPRESSED)

1920s → ELECTRONIC INSTRUMENTS

  • 1928: Ondes Martenot created sound from vacuum tubes.


    • Image: Jonny Greenwood playing an Ondes Martenot — CC BY 2.0 (via Wikimedia Commons)

1950s → PROGRAMMABLE SYNTH + SOFTWARE SOUND

  • 1957: RCA Mark II synthesiser: programmable via paper tape; installed at Columbia University.
  • 1957: Bell Labs: Max Mathews writes MUSIC I (sound generation language), later MUSIC II adds four-voice polyphony.


Image: Max Mathews — CC BY-SA 2.0 (via Wikimedia Commons)

1960s → RESEARCH CULTURE

1960s → RESEARCH CULTURE

  • Composers visit Bell Labs (e.g., Jean Claude Risset, Charles Dodge); university labs proliferate; workflows are heavy/tedious with big machinery and tape transfer.

1970s → CENTRES FORM

  • Key centres listed: MIT; University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign; UC San Diego; Stanford University CCRMA.

1980s → FM SYNTH GOES MAINSTREAM

  • FM synthesis: John Chowning develops FM synthesis; the Yamaha DX7 becomes a prominent commercial FM synth; your deck claims a large share of 1986 #1 singles used it.


Image: Yamaha DX7 — CC BY 2.0 (via Wikimedia Commons)

1990s → NOW → SOFTWARE ECOSYSTEMS

  • Visual programming and live performance tools become standard, especially Max/MSP.
  • AI-era tools emerge (sample indexing, timbre transformation).
